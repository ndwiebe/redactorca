# PRODUCT — RedactorCA

**register:** brand

## Product purpose
A local-first tool that strips client identity out of a document — in the browser, before an accountant pastes it into ChatGPT / Copilot / Claude. The promise: **your client's data never leaves your computer.** 100% client-side, no server, no upload. Canadian-tax-aware (SIN, Business Number + program accounts, trust numbers, provincial health numbers, etc.). Dollar amounts are kept on purpose — the accountant wants the AI to read the figures, just not who they belong to.

## Users
Canadian CPAs and bookkeepers. Non-technical. Nervous about AI touching client files because their #1 constraint is data residency / confidentiality. They are not impressed by "tech"; they are reassured by discretion, precision, and obvious safety. They work in daylight, at a desk, under professional-liability pressure.

## Brand voice (three physical words)
**Discreet. Exacting. Reassuring.** The feeling of handing a sensitive file to a careful professional who quietly blacks out the right lines and hands it back. Plainspoken, not salesy. Confident, not loud.

## The motif
The **redaction bar** — a solid ink rectangle over text — is the identity. It is literally what the product does, it is iconic, and no competitor owns it. The logo, the hero, the loading state, the favicon all descend from the black bar.

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)
- Generic SaaS dashboard (what v0.1 looked like — internal-tool dark mode).
- Fintech navy + gold. Crypto neon-on-black. The "hacker terminal" dark aesthetic.
- Stripe-minimal gradient SaaS. Editorial-magazine display-serif + italic.
- Anything that makes a CPA think "this is a developer toy."

## Strategic principles
- Trust is the product. Every screen must make "nothing leaves your machine" feel obviously, physically true.
- Calm over clever. A CPA under liability pressure wants quiet competence, not delight-for-its-own-sake.
- Show, don't tell: the redaction happening live IS the marketing.
